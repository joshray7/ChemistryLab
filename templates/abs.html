Acids, Bases and Salts

Acids, bases, and salts are important chemical substances commonly used in homes, industries, and laboratories.

🧪 Acids

An acid is a substance that produces hydrogen ions (H⁺) when dissolved in water. Common examples of acids include hydrochloric acid, sulfuric acid, and vinegar.

Acids usually have a sour taste and can turn blue litmus paper red.

🧼 Bases

A base is a substance that produces hydroxide ions (OH⁻) when dissolved in water. Examples include sodium hydroxide, calcium hydroxide, and soap solutions.

Bases often feel slippery and can turn red litmus paper blue.

🧂 Salts

A salt is formed when an acid reacts with a base in a process called neutralization. Common salt (sodium chloride) is an example.

💼 Uses of Acids, Bases and Salts
  • Acids are used in batteries and food processing.
  • Bases are used in soap making and cleaning agents.
  • Salts are used in food preservation and medicine.
📊 Types of Acids, Bases and Salts

Acids, bases, and salts can be classified into different types based on their properties and compositions.


  • Acids: Strong acids (e.g., hydrochloric acid(HCl), sulfuric acid(H2SO4), nitric acid(HNO3)) and weak acids (e.g., acetic acid(CH3COOH), carbonic acid(H2CO3)).

  • Bases: Strong bases (e.g., sodium hydroxide(NaOH), calcium hydroxide(Ca(OH)2), potassium hydroxide(KOH)) and weak bases (e.g., ammonia(NH3), methylamine(CH3NH2), pyridine(C5H5N)).

  • Salts: Normal salts (e.g., sodium chloride(NaCl), potassium chloride(KCl)), acidic salts (e.g., sodium bisulfate(NaHSO4), calcium bisulfate(Ca(HSO4)2)), and basic salts (e.g., copper hydroxide chloride(Cu2(OH)2Cl2), calcium hydroxide chloride(Ca(OH)2Cl2)).
🔬 Laboratory Preparation

Acids, bases, and salts can be prepared in the laboratory through various chemical reactions. For example, hydrochloric acid can be prepared by reacting sodium chloride with sulfuric acid.

⚠️ Safety Note

Always handle acids, bases, and salts with care and follow proper safety protocols to prevent accidents and injuries.

Understanding acids, bases, and salts helps students understand chemical reactions and everyday chemical processes.
