Acid-Base Titration

Titration is a quantitative analytical technique used to determine the unknown concentration of a solution. An acid is carefully added to a base (or vice versa) until neutralization is complete — indicated by a color change from an indicator such as phenolphthalein.

01📖

Theory

In acid-base titration, a solution of known concentration (the standard solution) is used to determine the concentration of an unknown solution. The point at which the acid exactly neutralizes the base is called the equivalence point or end point.

An indicator is added to signal the end point. Phenolphthalein is pink in base and colorless in acid — the end point is when the pink color just disappears. Methyl orange is yellow in base and red in acid.

Neutralization equation HCl(aq) + NaOH(aq) → NaCl(aq) + H₂O(l)
Concentration formula C₁V₁ = C₂V₂ (for 1:1 mole ratio)
02🔧

Apparatus & Materials

Burette (50 cm³) Pipette (25 cm³) Conical flask Burette stand & clamp White tile Funnel 0.1M NaOH (standard) Unknown HCl solution Phenolphthalein indicator Distilled water Wash bottle
03📋

Procedure

1

Rinse the burette with distilled water, then with the NaOH solution. Fill the burette with 0.1M NaOH up to the 0.00 cm³ mark. Remove air bubbles from the tip.

2

Use the pipette to measure exactly 25 cm³ of the unknown HCl solution into a clean conical flask.

3

Add 2–3 drops of phenolphthalein indicator to the HCl in the conical flask. The solution should be colorless.

4

Place the conical flask on a white tile below the burette. Open the burette tap and add NaOH slowly, swirling the flask constantly.

5

As the end point approaches, add NaOH dropwise. Stop immediately when a faint pink color persists for 30 seconds — this is the end point.

6

Record the final burette reading. Calculate the volume of NaOH used (titre value).

7

Repeat the titration two more times to obtain concordant results (within 0.10 cm³ of each other).

04👁️

Observations & Results

Titration Initial Reading (cm³) Final Reading (cm³) Titre (cm³)
Rough0.0024.8024.80
1st0.0024.6024.60
2nd0.0024.5524.55
Average (concordant)——24.575 cm³
05🔢

Calculation

Volume of NaOH used (V₁): 24.575 cm³ = 0.024575 dm³
Concentration of NaOH (C₁): 0.1 mol/dm³
Volume of HCl (V₂): 25 cm³ = 0.025 dm³
Using C₁V₁ = C₂V₂ → C₂ = C₁V₁ ÷ V₂: (0.1 × 0.024575) ÷ 0.025 = 0.0983 mol/dm³
Concentration of HCl ≈ 0.098 mol/dm³
06🎯

Conclusion

The titration shows that 24.575 cm³ of 0.1M NaOH was required to neutralize 25 cm³ of the unknown HCl solution. Using the formula C₁V₁ = C₂V₂, the concentration of HCl was found to be approximately 0.098 mol/dm³. The end point was identified by a permanent faint pink color due to the phenolphthalein indicator.
