Testing for Acids and Bases Using Litmus Paper

In this experiment, we use litmus paper — a simple indicator — to identify whether a solution is acidic or basic. Acidic solutions turn blue litmus red, while basic solutions turn red litmus blue.

01📖

Theory

An acid is a substance that releases hydrogen ions (H⁺) when dissolved in water. Common examples include hydrochloric acid (HCl) and vinegar (ethanoic acid).

A base is a substance that releases hydroxide ions (OH⁻) in solution. Examples include sodium hydroxide (NaOH) and ammonia solution.

Litmus is a natural dye extracted from lichens. It exists in two forms — red and blue — and changes color in the presence of acids or bases, making it a simple and effective indicator.

02🔧

Apparatus & Materials

Red litmus paper Blue litmus paper Test tubes (x6) Test tube rack Dropper / Pipette Dilute HCl (acid) NaOH solution (base) Distilled water Vinegar Lemon juice Soap solution
03📋

Procedure

1

Label six test tubes: HCl, NaOH, water, vinegar, lemon juice, and soap solution.

2

Pour a small amount of each liquid into its labeled test tube using a dropper.

3

Dip a strip of blue litmus paper into the first test tube (HCl). Observe and record any color change.

4

Dip a strip of red litmus paper into the same test tube. Observe and record.

5

Repeat steps 3 and 4 for all remaining test tubes, using fresh litmus strips each time.

6

Record all observations in the table below.

05🎯

Conclusion

From this experiment, we conclude that acids turn blue litmus paper red while bases turn red litmus paper blue. Neutral substances such as distilled water cause no color change in either litmus paper. Litmus paper is therefore a reliable indicator for distinguishing acids from bases in a simple laboratory setting.
