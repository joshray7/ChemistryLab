Gas Laws

Boyle's Law

At constant temperature, the pressure of a gas is inversely proportional to its volume. i.e., P ∝ 1/V where V is the volume.

Charles's Law

At constant pressure, the volume of a gas is directly proportional to its absolute temperature. i.e., V ∝ T where T is the absolute temperature.

Avogadro's Law

At constant temperature and pressure, the volume of a gas is directly proportional to the number of moles. i.e., V ∝ n where n is the number of moles.

Ideal Gas Law

The relationship between pressure, volume, temperature, and number of moles of a gas is given by PV = nRT where P is the pressure, V is the volume, n is the number of moles, R is the ideal gas constant, and T is the absolute temperature.

Physical vs Chemical Changes

Physical Change

A physical change is a change in the state or appearance of a substance without altering its chemical composition. Examples include melting, freezing, and dissolving.

Chemical Change

A chemical change results in the formation of new substances with different properties. Examples include burning, rusting, and digestion.

Laws of Chemical Combination

Law of Conservation of Mass

Mass is neither created nor destroyed in a chemical reaction. The total mass of the reactants equals the total mass of the products.

Law of Definite Proportions

A given chemical compound always contains the same elements in the same proportion by mass, regardless of the source or method of preparation.

Law of Multiple Proportions

When two elements combine to form more than one compound, the masses of one element that combine with a fixed mass of the other are in ratios of small whole numbers.

Gay-Lussac's Law of Combining Volumes

When gases react together at constant temperature and pressure, the volumes of the reacting gases and the volumes of any gaseous products are in simple whole number ratios.

Laboratory Safety Rules

Wear Protective Gear

Always wear safety goggles, gloves, and a lab coat to protect yourself from chemical splashes and spills.

Know Emergency Procedures

Familiarize yourself with the location of safety equipment such as fire extinguishers, eye wash stations, and first aid kits.

Handle Chemicals Properly

Read labels carefully, use appropriate containers, and never mix chemicals unless instructed to do so.

Keep Your Workspace Clean

A tidy workspace helps prevent accidents. Clean up spills immediately and dispose of waste properly.
